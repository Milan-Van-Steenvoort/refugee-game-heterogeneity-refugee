There are two types of players in the decision game

  • Citizens
    • Citizens are participants who take part in a typing task where they have to fill in 5 sequences of numbers and letters and earn 25 or 75 points. All participants that are assigned the role of citizen perform the exact same task.
    • 40% of the points that are earned in the typing task are withheld and collected in a common account. You can think of these as taxes that citizens have to pay.
      • 40% of 75 points: 30 points
      • 40% of 25 points: 10 points
    • The remaining 60% are a citizen's personal earnings.
      • 60% of 75 points: 45 points
      • 60% of 25 points: 15 points
  • Refugee
    • The refugee does not participate in the typing task and therefore cannot contribute to the common account.
    • The refugee has no own income.

Each game consists of one refugee and two groups of four host citizens.

  • There are two groups of citizens: group A and group B.
  • Each group consists of 4 citizens.
  • After finishing the typing task, all citizens within a group earn the same amount of points, but earnings can differ between groups.
