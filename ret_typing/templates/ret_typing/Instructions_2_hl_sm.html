There are two types of players in the decision game

  • Citizens
    • Citizens are participants who take part in the typing task where you have to fill in 5 sequences of numbers and letters and earn 25 or 75 points (the task you just practiced).
    • There are 2 types of citizens:
      • High-earning citizens who earn 75 points in the typing task.
      • Low-earning citizens who earn 25 points in the typing task.
    • 40% of the points that are earned in the typing task are withheld and collected in a common account. You can think of these as taxes that citizens have to pay.
      • High-earning citizens: 30 points (40% of 75 points).
      • Low-earning citizens: 10 points (40% of 25 points).
    • The remaining 60% are a citizen's personal earnings
      • High-earning citizens: 45 points (60% of 75 points).
      • Low-earning citizens: 15 points (60% of 25 points).
  • Refugee
    • The refugee (another participant in this study) does not participate in the typing task and therefore cannot contribute to the common account.
    • The refugee has no own income.

Each game consists of one refugee and two groups of four host citizens.

  • Each group consists of 4 citizens.
  • Citizens in a same group receive identical earnings.
  • Whether you are a high-earning or low-earning citizen was decided randomly.
  • You have been assigned to a group of low-earning citizens.
  • The other group consists of citizens with high earnings from the typing task.
