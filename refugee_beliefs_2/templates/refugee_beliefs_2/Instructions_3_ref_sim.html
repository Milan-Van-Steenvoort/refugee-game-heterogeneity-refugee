Both groups of citizens have to decide how much should be given to the refugee.

  • The exact decisions to be made are similar for both groups, and are explained below
  • Participants from group A and participants from group B are making their decisions at the same time (simultaneously). This means that participants from group A do not know what participants from group B decide until after participants from group A have made their decision. Likewise, participants from group B do not know what participants from group A decide until after participants from group B have made their decision.

Each host citizen has to make the following (private and independent) decision:

  • The amount that a citizen can give to the refugee varies between 0% and 20% of the common account of that citizen's group.
  • To do this, the citizen needs to move a slider to the desired position. Example:
  • The slider varies between 0% and 20%, with 20% corresponding to one fifth of the common account of that citizen's group.
  • In each group, a citizen is chosen at random, whose decision determines how many points from the common account of that citizen's group are given to the refugee.
  • The remaining points not assigned to the refugee are divided equally between the citizens within their group.
  • Depending on how much is given to the refugee, the amount each citizen receives varies between 20% and 25% of the common account of that citizen's group.
